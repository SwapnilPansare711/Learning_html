RAIGAD

Captial Of Swarajya

Raigad Fort is a historic hill fort in Maharashtra. It is located in the Sahyadri mountain range. The fort stands about 820 meters above sea level. It was the capital of Chhatrapati Shivaji Maharaj. Shivaji Maharaj was crowned here in 1674. The fort has strong natural defenses. It is surrounded by steep cliffs. There is only one main entrance called Maha Darwaja. The fort includes the royal palace ruins. It also has the queen’s quarters. The Jagdishwar Temple is located inside the fort. Shivaji Maharaj’s samadhi is here. The fort has a large market area. There are water storage tanks on the fort. It played an important role in Maratha history. The fort was captured by the Mughals in 1689. Later, it came under British control. Today, it is a popular tourist destination. Visitors can reach it by steps or ropeway. Raigad Fort symbolizes pride and bravery of the Maratha Empire.
